#Thoughts in job searching process

I just completed my job searching journey, which span around 3 weeks and concluded with 6 offers. Leading to it was a major haul effort of 110 applications to companies in Vietnam, SEA, Europe and Australia, 38 interviews participated, 12 tests taken and more than a handful of rejections. In this post I will dive deeper and give my own insight about this rather exhausting process and job market for data analyst in Vietnam.

0. Background and disclaimer
Like I said, I'm a data analyst with around 2 YOE and a fairly solid background in finance. My last workplace is a software outsourcing company, so we had exposure to many tech stacks and different domains. With my experience, I aim for positions like: data analyst, analytics engineer or data engineer with a heavier side of ETL managing. Regarding of domain, I'm open to any possible domains with a preference of finance-related products. 

Here is the hiring process of most companies I applied to:
1/ Application submission
2/ Screening call
3/ Test
4/ First interview
5/ Test
6/ Second interview
7/ Offer/reject

Most companies goes with 4 steps (1-3-4-7). The test can be optional and mostly required before the first interview, as a way to deload for the interviewer. 

In term of company sizes, most of the companies I applied to has a team size of 51-100 employees. Only a few of them allow the remote working method.
